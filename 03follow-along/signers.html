Notable Signers of the Declaration of Independence

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence; later served as the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Prominent advocate for independence and diplomat; later served as the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned statesman, inventor, writer, and diplomat; one of the most famous and respected leaders of the American Revolution.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress at the time of signing; famous for his large, bold signature that became synonymous with the word "signature."
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Political organizer and leader of colonial resistance; a key figure in the movement toward independence often called a "Father of the American Revolution."
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the formal resolution for independence to the Continental Congress in June 1776.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration and the last surviving signer (died 1832).
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Notable statesman who signed several key American founding documents; the only person to sign the Articles of Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
